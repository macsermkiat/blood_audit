## Acute-blood-loss override

The override is the only pathway that bypasses the Hb threshold and only
when **all** of these are documented:

- A procedure-proximal anchor datetime (active surgical or procedural
  episode).
- A delta-Hb that meets one of:
  - ≥ 1.5 g/dL drop within 6 hours
  - ≥ 2.0 g/dL drop within 12 hours
  - ≥ 2.5 g/dL drop within 24 hours

Vital-sign abnormalities alone (low BP, tachycardia) do not trigger the
override.

## Exclusions baked into the audit

- Pediatric patients (age < 15) — different policy applies.
- Non-RBC products (LPRC, LDPRC, SDR).
- Orders without a documented pre-transfusion Hb.
- ICD-10 conditions that take the patient out of scope per PR 17.2.

PR 17.2 is an internal hospital document. To respect institutional
