## In one paragraph

blood_audit reviews inpatient adult red blood cell (RBC) and platelet
transfusions at Chulalongkorn Hospital, after they are complete. It reads
HOSxP exports from the hospital information system. For each audited
order it records one of five core classifications — `APPROPRIATE`,
`INAPPROPRIATE`, `NEEDS_REVIEW`, `INSUFFICIENT_EVIDENCE`, or
`POTENTIALLY_INAPPROPRIATE` — or one of four routing/reservation
terminals: `RETURNED_NOT_TRANSFUSED` and `PERIOP_TRANSFUSION_EXEMPT`
(units returned or declared surgical use; excluded from appropriateness
scoring), `PREOP_OVER_RESERVATION` (more units reserved than the MSBOS
recommendation for the planned operation), and
`PREOP_RESERVATION_UNCONFIRMED` (flag-gated, default-off).
The appropriateness decision follows the KCMH internal
guideline PR 17.2 (Procedure Revision 17.2) and the AABB 2023
(Association for the Advancement of Blood & Biotherapies) standard,
using a 3-tier hemoglobin (Hb) policy with clinical-context thresholds
and evidence-gated bypasses. In developer docs these context buckets are
called "cohorts"; they are per-order audit labels, not permanent patient
groups.

Rows that the deterministic classifier routes to the LLM go through six
fail-closed quote-grounding layers. This route includes unclear clinical
contexts, gray-zone Hb rows, high-Hb rows, and safety-flag rows that should
not be auto-classified from the Hb number alone.
"LLM_REVIEW" is a routing concept inside the pipeline; it is not a
stored classification. A failed grounding becomes
`final_classification = NEEDS_REVIEW` with `review_reason` set, so a
human reviewer can adjudicate.

## What it is not

- **Not Software as a Medical Device.** Nothing in this codebase influences
  a transfusion order at the point of care.
- **Not real-time.** No subscriber to the audit stream emits clinician
  alerts. No POC dashboard pushes recommendations.
- **Not a single-physician scorecard.** Per-physician views exist for the
  reviewing clinician's own audit (own-view layout); they are not a
  quality scorecard for management review.

If anyone asks for real-time CDS hooks, decline. That would move the
project into SaMD territory and change everything: regulatory scope,
review processes, deployment risk profile.

## What it produces

A monthly audit dataset for the QI committee. From this dataset, six
report sections are derived:

- Hospital-trend summary.
- Ward-scorecard.
- Physician own-view (per reviewer).
- Indication breakdown.
- Cohort breakdown.
- Health-state breakdown.

The report schema lives in [`docs/report-schema.md`](https://github.com/macsermkiat/blood_audit/blob/main/docs/report-schema.md)
and is summarized at [Report output](/blood_audit/en/clinical/report-output/).

## Anti-hallucination guarantees

For rows that the deterministic classifier routes to LLM review, the
LLM's clinical reasoning is gated by
[six fail-closed quote-grounding layers](/blood_audit/en/developers/quote-grounding/).
A failed grounding stores `final_classification = NEEDS_REVIEW` with
`review_reason = "hallucination_suspect"`; it is not published as an
`APPROPRIATE` or `INAPPROPRIATE` result.
