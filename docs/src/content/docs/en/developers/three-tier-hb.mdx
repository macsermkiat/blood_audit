## The acute-blood-loss override

The override is the only pathway that bypasses the Hb threshold. It fires
only when **all** of these are documented:

- Procedure-proximal anchor datetime (within an active surgical or
  procedural episode).
- Delta-Hb meets one of:
  - ≥ 1.5 g/dL drop within 6 hours
  - ≥ 2.0 g/dL drop within 12 hours
  - ≥ 2.5 g/dL drop within 24 hours

Vital signs (low BP, high HR) are **supporting evidence only**. They never
override the Hb tier on their own.

## Why vitals are not standalone

Hemoglobin is the published threshold in KCMH PR 17.2 and AABB 2023.
Vital-sign-driven transfusion is a discretionary clinical judgment outside
the audit's deterministic scope. Vitals enter the LLM evidence bundle for
Tier 3 reasoning; they do not change the deterministic classification.

## Cohort thresholds

| Cohort | Hb threshold for `APPROPRIATE` |
| --- | --- |
| ICU | ≤ 7.0 g/dL |
| Non-ICU (medicine, surgery, oncology) | ≤ 8.0 g/dL |

Cohort assignment comes from `bba.cohort_detector` and uses procedure-code
proximity, ward, and admission service.

## Where this lives in code

- `src/bba/deterministic_classifier/` — the rule engine.
- `src/bba/cohort_detector/` — cohort assignment.
- Policy text (paraphrased above): see KCMH PR 17.2 and the internal
